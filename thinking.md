# Thinking

On AI, the evolution of prompts, Skills, and the "old Chinese medicine doctor" as a direction.

---

## I. The Complete Picture of AI

Artificial Intelligence. We respond with fear or greed, but AI will ultimately come with its own wisdom.

Today's LLMs come with a vast built-in knowledge base and, through techniques like reinforcement learning, have learned a wide range of thinking methods and skills. Meanwhile, humans provide AI with the hands and feet to perceive and manipulate the world through Tools, MCP, and Skills.

You could say the LLM itself comes with a nearly "unbeatable" knowledge base, a reasoning brain, hands and feet (Tools, MCP, Skills, etc.), and finally achieves goals through context and prompts (user-provided, system-provided — Skills can also function as prompts).

## III. The Evolution of Prompts: From Exhaustive to "Appropriate"

Looking back at the development of LLMs — GPT-3, early Stable Diffusion models — at the beginning we had to patiently and explicitly describe positive requirements and negative prompts to get decent results.

As models' natural language processing and reasoning capabilities improved, these requirements seemed to fade, but in reality they remain hidden in underlying rules.

Detailed prompts that once helped have become a constraint on LLMs — with "appropriate" prompting, the model can perhaps do better than humans. And the word "appropriate" is extremely subtle: like "适量" (appropriate amount) in Chinese cooking, or the因人用量 (dosage varies by person) in Chinese medicine.

---

## IV. The "Old Chinese Medicine Doctor" Direction

How to become an "old Chinese medicine doctor" in the AI era — that's an interesting and potentially effective direction.

The core skill of the old doctor isn't how many prescriptions they've memorized:
- Knowing which prescription fits the moment
- Knowing the right dosage (it varies by person)
- Knowing when to stop and when to adjust

Applied to using AI, the competition isn't about who can write longer prompts, but who better understands "appropriateness" — giving the right constraints at the right time, and letting go at the right time.

---

## V. From Principles to Skills

Returning to Skills themselves: the current formulations basically cover the core characteristics of LLMs. They can be structured more clearly:

1. **Set the goal**: Understand what needs to be done, think about how to verify success
2. **Plan carefully**: Think it through, then execute
3. **Stay focused**: Only change what needs changing
4. **Simple, not flashy**: No over-engineering
